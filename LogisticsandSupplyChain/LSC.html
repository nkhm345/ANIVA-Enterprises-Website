Strategic Logistics & Supply Chain Design

How It Works
  1. Perform Current State Analysis
    • Gather, analyze and validate data
    • Gather data on existing warehouse locations / operations
    • Gather warehouse / DC operating policies / constraints
    • Gather shipment flow data and (available) pertinent costs
    • Explore opportunities for consolidation or deconsolidation
  1. Perform Network Redesign
    • Optimize the number and locations of warehouses
    • Based on current supply base and demand points
    • Based on product flows & optimum opportunities
    • Generate optimum allocation of products to warehouses
    • Review & refine final design based on stake-holder feedback
  1. Develop an Implementation Cadence and Timeline
Data Requirements
  1. Facility Data
    • Current warehouse location data (Address, DUNS code, Latitude / Longitude codes)
    • Current supply locations (Physical address, DUNS Code, Latitude / Longitude codes)
    • Current destination / customer / demand location data (same as above)
    • Operation parameters & constraints for the warehouses and customer locations
  1. Supply Chain Cost Data
    • Existing warehouse operating costs (fixed and variable costs)
    • Transportation costs from / to warehouses and other facilities
      • $ / mile or $ / hundred weight that may vary by facility or geographic regions
    • Value of products by type (inventory carrying value, supply chain pipeline costs)
    • Packaging and other costs (at product / shipment level, if available)
  1. Product Data
    • Type and number of products
    • Quantities / volume of products shipped from each supplier
    • Quantities / volume of products produced or required at the final customer location
  1. Capacity Limitations/Operation Restrictions
    • Warehouse sizes (square feet)
    • Weight, Volumes, SKUs and Other storage limitations
  1. Material Flow Data
    • Supplier to warehouses (who supplies to which warehouse and how much)
    • Warehouses to end customer (warehouse to customer and how much)
      • Weight, Volume, Number of truckloads or products moving in any other modes
  1. Other supply chain design data
    • Preferred Warehouse / Candidate sites and pertinent data

Sample Strategic Supply Chain Analysis

  • Origin-based network design vs. destination-based network design
  • Integrated inbound transportation, warehousing, production planning & inventory control
  • Supplier / customer assignment to cross docks / warehouses / distribution centers to reduce transportation costs
  • Multi-plant / Multi-warehouse drop-off network design
  • Truck vs. rail optimization between suppliers, warehouses & customers
  • Optimize number & location of warehouses / DC's / cross docks

Tactical Logistics & Supply Chain Design

How It Works
  1. Map the current state logistics processes and understand the gap
  1. Develop the scope and gather data
  1. Study existing 3PL / core carriers and estimate current inbound route costs
  1. Get complete control of inbound shipment data from suppliers and review all inbound shipments for consolidation opportunities
  1. Understand and analyze the inbound logistics practices and identify opportunities for optimization (route, cube, mode)
    • Use an industry-standard route optimization software
    • Pick constraints and optimization time buckets judiciously
      • Production constraints
      • Demand / supply constraints
      • Equipment capacity constraints
      • Facility storage availability
      • Carrier restrictions / DOT regulations
      • Backhaul opportunities
      • Carrier options / constraints, etc.
  1. Conduct route optimization using commercial logistics planning software
  1. Map the proposed state logistics processes
  1. Implement the proposed network design and logistics processes
  1. Perform ongoing continuous improvement studies to ensure that the inbound costs are within the budget
    • Cube improvement and route redesigns
    • Frequency and mode changes
    • Multi-plant drop off
    • Review and revise the logistics operating rules
    • Constraint elimination at supply and delivery locations
  1. Measure and report key performance metrics
    • Operational metrics (daily, weekly, quarterly, yearly)
    • Financial metrics (daily, weekly, quarterly, yearly)
    • Compliance metrics (3PL, plants, suppliers)
