Focus on logistics, transportation and supply chain consulting, cost reduction projects, performance measurement and business process management services through relentless customer interface and supplier/service provider collaboration

  • We work closely with the leadership and several functional groups and develop operational strategies, process redesigns, benchmarks, strategic and tactical supply chain/logistics network solutions in addition to setting up new capabilities (people, process, systems and tools)

About the President & Founder

Dr. Mani Manivannan has 28 years of technical and executive leadership experience in both industry and academia (in US and abroad). His areas of expertise include supply chain strategy, logistics network optimization, warehousing & distribution, subassembly, sequencing & kitting, process documentation & compliance, supply chain metrics & KPI measurements, facilities design, materials handling, demand forecasting, database design, inventory control, and total landed cost management.

As part of ANIVA Enterprises, he has consulted with several clients in the US. He has 20 years of work experience at various capacities in large US corporations involved in manufacturing, transportation/logistics and supply chain. He was formerly a faculty member in the School of Industrial and Systems Engineering at Georgia Tech and published numerous articles in prestigious journals and conference proceedings.

He was a recipient of 1991 Outstanding Young Manufacturing Engineer of the Year (National) Award from SME, 1996 Raymond F. O'Brien Award of Excellence from the Con-Way Board of Directors, 2002 Speed Demon Award from the CEO of GM and 2010 Nunn-Perry Award from the Department of Defense (DoD) and two Chairman's awards at BAE Systems. He has served as an adjunct Associate Professor in the School of Business at Oregon Graduate Institute and in the School of Management at Portland State University, a panel member at National Science Foundation (NSF), and the Program Chair for the WSC '98 Conference. He received his Ph.D. in Industrial Engineering from Pennsylvania State University in 1988, BS in Mechanical Engineering and MS in Management from India, and MS in Industrial Engineering and Operations Research from Syracuse University.
